# I am Faiza Jabeen

**Statistician · M.Phil Statistics, University of the Punjab**

I work on the part of data analysis that doesn't make it into the demo: the messy sensor file, the missing values that aren't missing at random, the model that looks brilliant until you fix the validation split.

My M.Phil research integrated machine learning with spatial methods to model groundwater contamination across Pakistan. The finding that stayed with me — that ordinary kriging beat XGBoost until spatial features were added, at which point the ordering flipped — is a fair summary of how I think about modelling: **complexity should be earned, not assumed.**

### Projects

**[Retail Analytics Dashboard](https://github.com/faiza-jabeen/retail-analytics-dashboard)**
Two years of e-commerce transactions turned into a dashboard a business could actually run on: revenue, margin, AOV, cohort retention, and RFM segmentation. The headline finding — at-risk customers hold 21% of revenue against 7% from new customers — says retention spend beats acquisition.
`Python` · `pandas` · `Chart.js`

**[Pakistan Development Indicators](https://github.com/faiza-jabeen/pakistan-development-indicators)**
35 years of World Bank data, pulled live from the API, modelled in SQL, and benchmarked against regional peers. Remittances from overseas Pakistanis are 13× larger than all foreign investment combined. Bangladesh, poorer than Pakistan in 1990, overtook it in 1998.
`SQL` · `SQLite` · `window functions` · `Python`

**[Lahore Air Quality](https://github.com/faiza-jabeen/lahore-air-quality)**
PM2.5 across five monitoring stations, implemented twice — once in Python, once in R. Linear regression beat Random Forest, and the README explains why that's the most interesting result in the repo.
`Python` · `R` · `scikit-learn` · `tidyverse`
